## Steps

**Data Preprocessing**

- You may follow your plan for preparing the dataset.
- You may need more instructions on specific techniques like normalization (scaling
    values to a range, e.g., 0–1) and standardization (transforming data to have mean 0
    and standard deviation 1). Your TA will explain these in Lab 6.
- Keep a clear record of every step: what transformations were applied, why they were
    necessary, and any assumptions made. This documentation will be part of your
    report and helps ensure reproducibility.

**Training the Model**

- Begin with a baseline model such as Linear Regression, Logistic Regression, or
    Decision Tree. This gives you a reference point for performance.
- Optionally, add advanced models for comparison if time permits.
- Use libraries available on lab computers, such as scikit-learn or PyTorch, for
    implementation.
- If you have prior experience with data science, you may split your data into training
    set and testing set, but this step is not required for this course. However, you may
    ask your TA what it is and why we need to do this.
- Document your process clearly so others can follow your workflow.

**Evaluate Your Model**

- Classification tasks: Use Accuracy (e.g., how many predictions were correct out of
    total cases).
- Regression tasks: Use least-squared error.


- Clustering: Check cluster cohesion and separation visually (scatter plots or cluster
    diagrams).
- Dimension Reduction: Check whether the reduced dimensions preserve the
    structure of the data visually using 2D or 3D scatter plots.
- You may use additional metrics not listed above, but you must be able to explain
    what they measure and why they are appropriate to the TA and classmates.
- If you used multiple models, compare their performance and highlight differences
    between baseline and refined models.

**Compare models (If you used different models. Optional)**

- Compare their performance and highlight differences between baseline and refined
    models.

**Visualize results**

- Choose a clear and meaningful way to present model performance (e.g., bar charts
    for accuracy, scatter plots for regression predictions).

**Interpret findings**

- Explain what the metrics mean for your problem.
- Discuss any limitations (e.g., small dataset size).
- Any ethics problem in the interpretation
